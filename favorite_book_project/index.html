Blood at the Root

Blood at the Root by LaDarrion Williams is a contemporary fantasy novel that blends magic, history, and Black culture. The story follows Malik, a 17-year-old with extraordinary powers, as he discovers his family legacy and navigates a magical university deeply rooted in African, Caribbean, and Black American traditions. This debut novel weaves elements of fantasy, identity, and resilience into an emotional and thrilling adventure.

Book Summary

In Blood at the Root, Malik is a 17-year-old who has spent most of his life running from his past after his mother disappeared and he discovered his uncontrollable magical abilities. He’s been trying to protect his foster brother, Taye, while hiding from the dangers of his powers. Everything changes when he discovers Caiman University, a hidden magical HBCU that his mother once attended. Here, Malik uncovers his family’s secrets, the roots of his magic, and the ancient forces threatening his community.

About the Author

LaDarrion Williams is a playwright and author who draws inspiration from Black culture, history, and mythology. Blood at the Root is his debut novel and showcases his ability to blend the fantastical with the deeply personal experiences of Black communities. Williams' work often reflects themes of family, identity, and resilience, making him a rising voice in contemporary fantasy literature.

Why I Like This Book

I love Blood at the Root for its rich blend of magic, history, and cultural depth. The story of Malik navigating his powers and identity resonated with me, particularly because of how the novel beautifully intertwines Black culture and historical themes with a contemporary fantasy setting. The characters are well-developed, and the exploration of heritage and self-discovery adds a compelling emotional layer to the fantasy elements.
